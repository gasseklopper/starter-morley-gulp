Understand the user needs

reading reddit and research communitys are essential user research. closed beta interviews, and looking lets plays could tell you alot of your user interface or stash managment usabillity.

Design the solution and show up problems

Great User Experiences start with sturdy concept and a clear stradegy. testing iterations of prototypes, provides the foundation of a great UX Product. before starting the "Design process" the UX flow must be discoverd and tested by a larger audience. At this point feedback is the game changer. Interviews and testings can make the produkt a serious Product on launch. The optimization of customer journeys provides me the parts for going the next step, of user centrified design. All the effort in unwanted features can be used, to make wanted features work as they should from user needs and not from designers preference behaivor.

Build the product

Unifying the concepts with technological architecture needs is the final step, turning the effort to a final feature of users needs. platform adjustments, scalability and optimize performance is the key for building the most effective and efficient solution. This convergence of UX research and design will make all customers happy.

UX-Design is in my DNA.

From photoshop 7.0, adobe xd, adobe experience manager(AEM), creative cloud 2020, writing javascript, speaking with api's, and develop large scale web/app projects. all these activitys are used with a focus being effective and efficient, working with UI from given tools is a 24/7 challenge for me to get more effective.

The last 3 years i worked as a UX/designer at Comwrap in Frankfurt. Building Prototypes, concepting websites, developing atomic design frontend components for enterprise CMS systems.

Also my 15 Years Experience as a photographer for high end commercials, teached me how important it is that something visual have to tell you large but clear story in seconds. i can apply these to User interfaces
